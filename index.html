Designed for industry

Built for construction sites and factory floors — not labs.

Every design decision in SmartStep reflects real deployment constraints: older ruggedised Android devices, gloved hands, loud environments, and workers who shouldn't need a manual to stay safe.

Construction

Lone workers on site after hours

A confirmed slip or fall triggers an immediate GPS-located emergency alert to designated contacts. The V12 brief's oil rig night-time slip scenario — a lone worker with no way to call for help — is the product's north star.

Manufacturing

Fatigue accumulation across a 12-hour shift

Gait asymmetry, COM displacement, and Minimum Toe Clearance all degrade before a single incident occurs. SmartStep flags the drift early — giving H&S managers a window to intervene before the risk becomes a statistic.

Underground & tunnelling

No signal, no gaps in the safety record

When the app detects lost connectivity, it commands the MCU to cache all incoming sensor data locally. The moment the connection resumes, the session is compiled and synced — preserving a complete RIDDOR-compliant record.

How it works

Seven layers. One insole. Unlimited shifts.

SmartStep is removable and fits all V12 boot models. No retooling, no replacement boots, and no per-user calibration — V12's pre-loaded sole geometry handles that.

01

Sense

The FSR pressure matrix and 6-axis IMU capture plantar load and full kinematic data at up to 100 Hz. Micro-thermistors and humidity sensors monitor foot temperature and moisture across the full shift.

02

Process

The onboard MCU fuses all sensor data to compute gait metrics and classify accidents at the edge — before anything is sent to the phone. This keeps BLE bandwidth low and battery life long.

03

Alert

A haptic buzzer above the metatarsal fires within 2 seconds of a detected threat. It's the physical last resort — a warning that reaches the worker even when audio and visual cues can't.

04

Report

At shift end the app compiles gait data, incident coordinates, and kinematic precursor metrics into a RIDDOR-compliant payload and syncs to Firestore — ready for H&S root cause analysis.

Physical design

Seven layers, engineered from the sole up.

SmartStep integrates into V12's existing Bison IGS VR600.01 platform. Each layer was selected or upgraded to serve both comfort and safety-critical sensing — and every component is removable for maintenance.

Layer breakdown

From the foot down.

1
Top surface

Moisture-wicking & absorption layer

High-density self-regulating absorption pads cover the inner lining. Waterproofs the electronics beneath, regulates temperature, and reduces bacterial and fungal build-up during prolonged wear.

2
Sensing

Biocompatible pressure matrix

High-resolution FSR array placed directly beneath the moisture layer. Maps full plantar load distribution at 60–120 FPS. Flexible, lightweight polymer construction ensures biocompatibility during prolonged skin contact.

3
Intelligence

Core electronics architecture

6-axis IMU, GPS, BLE radio, MCU, micro-thermistors and humidity sensors — miniaturised onto an IC and encapsulated in flexible potting compound for shock protection. Arch-mounted for comfort. Removable via pogo pin connectors.

4
Protection

Non-metallic composite midsole

Shields feet and the insole layers from sharp object penetration. Non-metallic by design — standard steel midsoles would interfere electromagnetically with the onboard sensors and cause packet loss from EMI.

5
Warning

Haptic buzzer module

Positioned above the metatarsal for maximum sensitivity and minimum comfort impact. Fires within 2 seconds of a confirmed gait-based injury threat — the physical failsafe when audio and visual alerts can't reach the worker.

6
Power

Li-Ion battery & cable routing

Rechargeable cell housed in the water-resistant tongue, accessible behind the V12 logo zip. Pre-existing bellows tongue routes cabling to electronics without structural modification. Sized for a full 12-hour shift.

7
Outsole

IGS tread — geometrically optimised

V12's proprietary IGS sole re-evaluated for tread edge geometry and material to maximise grip in adverse conditions. Weight additions minimised; shock-resistance classifications preserved. Pre-loaded geometry eliminates per-user calibration.

Electronics

What's inside the IC

  • 6-axis IMU

    3-axis accelerometer + gyroscope. Captures full kinematic data per stride. ZUPT correction eliminates drift across a 12-hour shift.

  • FSR pressure matrix

    Biocompatible array delivering plantar load heatmaps at 60–120 FPS. .

  • GPS module

    Triangulates user location for geofencing and incident coordinate logging. GPS writes directly to Firestore, independent of BLE telemetry.

  • Micro-thermistors & humidity sensors

    Monitor foot and electronics temperature continuously. App alerts within 5 seconds when temperature exceeds 43 °C — protecting both the worker and the device.

  • BLE 5.0 radio

    Throttled to 0.1–0.2 Hz during normal operation to preserve battery. Escalates immediately on incident detection, pushing a 10-second kinematic snapshot to the app.

Design principles

Designed around the worker, not the engineer.

Every UI decision was validated against real-world deployment constraints — not ideal conditions. The app must work on older ruggedised Androids, in loud environments, with gloved hands, and for workers who've never used a safety app before.

Small-screen first

All tap targets ≥ 48×48 dp, sized for gloved hands on 360 dp-width devices. 78% of construction workers use personal phones — many carry older, smaller-screen Androids.

High contrast & haptic-first

All text/background contrast ratios exceed WCAG AA (>4.5:1). Haptic alerts fire within 2 seconds — the physical fallback when audio and visual cues can't reach the worker on a noisy factory floor.

Sub-100ms navigation

All page transitions complete within 100 ms. When a worker urgently navigates to pressure metrics or incident data in a safety-critical situation, front-end buffering is a direct risk — not an annoyance.

GDPR & RIDDOR compliant

Sign-up includes a GDPR consent notice. Incident metadata — timestamp, GPS coordinates, mechanism — is captured in a format exportable to the HSE within the mandatory 10-day reporting window.

3-minute onboarding

Plain-English labels and minimal required inputs. A representative non-technical user must complete first login and insole pairing within 3 minutes without assistance — this is a hard acceptance criterion, not an aspiration.

Offline-resilient

On connectivity loss the insole caches all sensor data locally via Hive. On reconnection the app immediately compiles and syncs the full session — no gaps in the worker's safety history, even underground.

Detection science

Six biomechanical metrics. Every step. Every shift.

SmartStep's detection suite draws on the CDC/NIOSH Lockhart report, the MDPI 2024 systematic review, and Fehr et al. 2024 (Nature). Each metric targets a distinct failure mode in the gait cycle.

HCV — Heel Contact Velocity

Primary slip indicator. Elevated heel velocity at initial contact correlates strongly with traction loss. The CDC/NIOSH Lockhart report identifies this as the most reliable real-time slip precursor available from wearable sensing.

RCOF — Required Coefficient of Friction

Quantifies the friction demand placed on the floor surface per stride. Threshold exceedances predict slip onset and enable proactive surface risk flagging — useful for identifying hazardous floor zones before they cause an incident.

MTC — Minimum Toe Clearance

The trip-specific metric. MTC narrows progressively with fatigue — making it the earliest detectable indicator of trip risk across a long shift. Validated by Fehr et al. 2024 (Nature) and the MDPI 2024 systematic review.

ZUPT — Zero Velocity Update

Corrects IMU drift during the stance phase, enabling accurate stride length and cadence estimates across a 12-hour shift. Without ZUPT, position-dead-reckoning errors accumulate and corrupt fatigue metrics over time.

SDI / SDII — Slip Distance

Quantifies forward and lateral foot displacement post-heel contact. Distinguishes recoverable stumbles from genuine fall-risk events — critical for maintaining worker trust by minimising false positive alerts.

COM — Centre of Mass displacement

Tracks whole-body balance degradation over a shift. An early-warning signal for fall risk that precedes any discrete event — giving H&S managers a window to intervene proactively before a threshold is breached.

Incident response

From detection to documented — automatically.

RIDDOR requires employers to report specified injuries to the HSE within 10 days. SmartStep generates the full evidentiary record the moment an event occurs — timestamped, GPS-precise, and immutable from the client.

On incident detection

  • Haptic alert fires (< 2 s)

    Buzzer module above the metatarsal triggers on both insole and smartphone — the physical failsafe in high-noise environments where audio and visual cues fail.

  • 10-second kinematic snapshot captured

    MCU accumulates the surrounding gait data — HCV, COM, MTC, bilateral asymmetry — and pushes a structured burst to the app via BLE at 100 Hz transfer rate.

  • Emergency contacts alerted (< 10 s)

    Designated contacts receive a GPS-located alert automatically. This directly addresses the HSE's 35 fatal falls from height in 2024/25 — many involving lone workers with no way to call for help.

  • RIDDOR-compliant record created

    Firestore entry written with server-side timestamp, GPS coordinates, mechanism classification, and full kinematic precursor suite — immutable from the client and exportable within the 10-day reporting window.

What the manager sees

  • HCV / COM chart

    Time-series view of heel contact velocity and centre of mass displacement across the incident window — revealing the biomechanical build-up before the event occurred.

  • Bilateral MTC chart

    Left-right toe clearance comparison across the preceding strides. Progressive narrowing on one side is a reliable trip precursor and supports root cause analysis.

  • Stride asymmetry chart

    Gait symmetry score across the shift — revealing whether the incident followed a period of progressive fatigue accumulation that could have been intercepted.

  • Incident heatmap & GPS coordinates

    Precise incident location plotted on the organisational map. Repeated incidents in the same zone automatically elevate it as a geofenced high-risk area for all workers on site.
