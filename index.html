Core capabilities

Everything your H&S team needs. In the sole of a boot.

Six biomechanical metrics, a dual-user dashboard, and emergency escalation — all without changing your existing V12 boot range.

Slip, Trip & Fall detection

Heel Contact Velocity, RCOF, and Minimum Toe Clearance computed on-device at up to 100 Hz to catch micro-events before escalation.

Fatigue monitoring

Gait asymmetry and ZUPT-corrected stride metrics flag progressive fatigue across a 12-hour shift, not just peak events.

Pressure matrix

FSR array maps plantar load distribution in real time — no per-user calibration required thanks to V12's pre-loaded sole geometry.

Dual dashboard

Individual workers see personal gait scores; H&S managers access an organisational Firestore-backed dashboard with team-wide RIDDOR reporting.

Emergency escalation

A confirmed fall triggers an automated alert to designated emergency contacts and the organisational dashboard within seconds via LTE.

Insole-only architecture

No phone required during activity. BLE syncs passively at 0.1–0.2 Hz; the insole's onboard Hive cache stores data across a full shift if connectivity drops.

How it works

From footstrike to dashboard in milliseconds.

01

Sense

The embedded 6-axis IMU and FSR matrix capture acceleration, angular velocity, and plantar pressure at up to 100 Hz during every footstrike.

02

Classify

On-device algorithms compute HCV, RCOF, MTC, COM displacement, and ZUPT-corrected stride data — flagging anomalies within the same gait cycle.

03

Sync

Data transmits via BLE to the SmartStep app, throttled to protect battery life. On STF detection, cadence escalates and LTE cloud upload triggers immediately.

04

Act

Workers receive personal feedback. Managers see live team risk scores. A confirmed fall auto-notifies emergency contacts and logs a RIDDOR-ready incident record.

The product

Built into the boot. Not bolted on.

SmartStep's commercial advantage is simple: V12's existing sole geometry is pre-loaded, eliminating the per-user scanning that competing systems require. Workers just insert the insole and go.

What's inside

  • 6-axis IMU

    3-axis accelerometer + 3-axis gyroscope capturing full kinematic data during every stride, with on-device signal processing to reduce BLE payload size.

  • FSR pressure matrix

    Force-sensitive resistor array mapped to the V12 sole geometry. Plantar pressure distribution is computed per-step without external calibration.

  • BLE 5.0 radio

    Low-energy Bluetooth maintains passive sync at 0.1–0.2 Hz during normal operation, escalating on STF detection events.

  • Hive local cache

    On-device NoSQL store buffers up to a full 12-hour shift of gait data if phone connectivity is lost — no data gaps at shift handover.

The technology

Six metrics. One insole. Zero compromise on safety.

SmartStep uses a biomechanically validated metric suite to detect the three leading causes of workplace injury: slips, trips, and falls.

Detection metric suite

Each metric targets a specific failure mode in the gait cycle.

  • HCV — Heel Contact Velocity

    Primary slip indicator. Elevated heel velocity at initial contact correlates strongly with loss of traction (Lockhart et al., CDC/NIOSH).

  • RCOF — Required Coefficient of Friction

    Quantifies the friction demand placed on the floor surface during each stride. A threshold exceedance predicts slip onset.

  • MTC — Minimum Toe Clearance

    The trip-specific analogue: MTC narrows progressively with fatigue. Sourced from MDPI 2024 systematic review and Fehr et al. 2024 (Nature).

  • ZUPT — Zero Velocity Update

    Corrects IMU drift during the stance phase, enabling accurate stride length and cadence estimates across a full shift.

  • SDI / SDII — Slip Distance

    Quantifies forward and lateral foot displacement post-heel contact, distinguishing recoverable stumbles from fall-risk events.

  • COM — Centre of Mass displacement

    Tracks whole-body balance degradation over a shift — an early indicator of fall risk before any discrete event occurs.

Why insole-only?

Competing systems (e.g., FallSafety Pro) rely on a phone being carried during activity. SmartStep's sensor-in-sole architecture means the detection layer is always present — regardless of where the worker's phone is.
