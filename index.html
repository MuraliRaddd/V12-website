Designed for industry

Built for construction sites and factory floors, not labs.

How it works

Seven layers. One insole. Unlimited shifts.

SmartStep is removable and fits all V12 boot models. No retooling, no replacement boots, and no per-user calibration. V12's pre-loaded sole geometry handles that.

01

Sense

Pressure sensors and a motion tracker capture load and movement data during every footstrike. Temperature and moisture sensors keep an eye on conditions throughout the full shift.

02

Process

The insole's onboard chip analyses all incoming data and identifies gait anomalies and safety events before anything is sent to the phone. This keeps the connection lightweight and the battery lasting longer.

03

Alert

A vibrating buzzer in the insole fires within seconds of a detected threat. It's the physical last resort, a warning that reaches the worker even when audio and visual cues can't.

04

Report

At shift end the app compiles gait data, incident locations, and movement context into a legally compliant record and syncs it to the cloud, ready for the H&S team to review.

Physical design

Seven layers, engineered from the sole up.

SmartStep integrates into V12's existing Bison boot platform. Each layer was selected or upgraded to serve both comfort and safety sensing, and every component can be removed for maintenance and replacement.

Layer breakdown

From the foot down.

1
Top surface

Moisture-wicking and absorption layer

High-density absorption pads line the inner surface. They protect the electronics below from moisture, regulate temperature during long shifts, and reduce the build-up of bacteria that comes with prolonged wear.

2
Sensing

Pressure matrix

A flexible grid of pressure sensors sits directly beneath the moisture layer, mapping how load is distributed across the foot with every step. The material is safe for long-term skin contact and light enough that you won't notice it's there.

3
Intelligence

Core electronics

Motion sensors, a location chip, wireless radio, and a processor are all packed into a single sealed unit and positioned under the arch where they cause the least discomfort. The unit clips out via magnetic connectors for easy removal.

4
Protection

Composite midsole

A non-metal plate between the insole and the outsole guards against sharp objects on the ground. The non-metal construction is intentional: a steel midsole would interfere with the wireless components above it.

5
Warning

Vibration alert module

A small vibrating unit sits above the ball of the foot. When the system detects a genuine threat, it buzzes the boot within seconds. On a loud factory floor where you can't hear your phone, this physical alert is often the only one that gets through.

6
Power

Battery and cabling

A rechargeable battery lives in the water-resistant tongue of the boot, tucked behind the V12 logo zip for easy access. Cabling runs from the battery to the electronics through an existing channel in the tongue, so no structural changes to the boot are needed.

7
Outsole

IGS tread geometry

V12's existing IGS sole has been re-evaluated for grip in wet and contaminated conditions. The tread shape and material have been refined to improve traction without adding noticeable weight or altering any of the boot's existing safety certifications.

Electronics

What's inside

The sealed electronics unit houses everything the insole needs to sense, process, and communicate, all in a package small enough to sit unnoticed beneath the arch of the foot.

  • 6-axis motion sensor

    Tracks acceleration and rotation across all three axes on every stride. A drift correction algorithm keeps the readings accurate across a full 12-hour shift.

  • Pressure sensing array

    Maps how weight is distributed across the foot in real time. The readings are clinically benchmarked to ensure the gait insights shown in the app are medically meaningful.

  • GPS chip

    Tracks the worker's location continuously for geofencing and for logging the precise coordinates of any incident. Location data is sent directly to the cloud, separately from the movement data.

  • Temperature and humidity sensors

    Monitor conditions inside the boot throughout the shift. If the temperature exceeds a safe threshold, the app alerts the worker within seconds, protecting both the wearer and the electronics.

  • Bluetooth radio

    Transmits data to the paired phone at a low rate during normal use to preserve battery life. When a safety event is detected, it switches to a rapid burst to get the data across immediately.

Design principles

Designed around the worker, not the engineer.

The app has to work in conditions where most consumer apps would fail. Bright sunlight on a screen. Thick gloves on a touchscreen. Loud machinery drowning out any notification sound. Older phones that haven't been updated in years. Every decision in the design was made with those realities in mind.

Works on any phone

All buttons and tap targets are sized for use with work gloves. The layout fits smaller and older screens without anything getting cut off or becoming hard to press.

Readable in any light

Text and background colours meet accessibility contrast standards. When the vibration alert fires, it acts as a physical backup for situations where the screen or audio simply can't get the worker's attention.

Instant response

Every screen loads immediately. If a worker needs to check their pressure readings or pull up an incident record in a hurry, a slow-loading page is not just annoying, it is a safety problem.

Legally compliant by default

Privacy consent is built into the sign-up flow. When an incident is logged, the app automatically captures the time, location and cause in a format that can be reported to the HSE within the required timeframe.

Up and running in 3 minutes

Plain language throughout, minimal steps, and no technical knowledge required. A first-time user with no experience of safety apps should be able to log in and pair their insole before their shift starts.

Works without signal

If the phone loses connection, the insole keeps recording and stores everything locally. When the connection comes back, the full session syncs automatically, so there are never any gaps in the safety record.

Safety technology

Every metric that matters, measured in every stride.

SmartStep monitors three interlocking systems continuously: how your feet move, how your weight is distributed, and where you are on site. Together, they give workers and safety managers a complete picture of risk, updated in real time throughout every shift.

Slip, trip and fall detection

Six kinematic metrics, each targeting a different way a worker can go down.

Heel Contact Velocity

The first thing the insole measures when the heel strikes the ground is how fast it hit. A hard, fast heel strike is the clearest early warning of a slip about to happen, well before the worker loses their footing.

Required Coefficient of Friction

Every stride places a friction demand on the floor surface. SmartStep tracks this demand continuously, flagging when it exceeds what most surfaces can safely provide. That threshold breach is where slips begin.

Minimum Toe Clearance

The gap between the toe and the ground during the swing phase of each step narrows gradually as fatigue sets in. SmartStep tracks this shrinkage stride by stride. A closing trend across a shift is the earliest indicator of trip risk there is.

Zero Velocity Update

Motion sensors drift over time. SmartStep corrects for this at every stance phase, keeping stride length and cadence readings accurate across a full 12-hour shift. Without this correction, fatigue readings become unreliable within hours.

Slip Distance

When the foot does slide, SmartStep measures how far it moved forward and sideways. This separates a minor stumble that the worker recovers from, from a genuine fall-risk event that needs escalating. Fewer false alarms means workers take real alerts seriously.

Centre of Mass Displacement

As the body's balance degrades over a long shift, the centre of mass shifts further from its ideal position with each step. SmartStep tracks this drift across the entire shift, giving H&S managers a heads-up before any single incident occurs.

Gait and pressure monitoring

A continuous picture of how the worker is holding up.

Beyond detecting incidents, SmartStep tracks six gait and pressure metrics throughout every shift. These paint a picture of fatigue accumulation long before it becomes dangerous, giving workers visibility of their own condition and managers the data they need to plan safe rotations.

Pressure

Live plantar pressure heatmap

A real-time colour map of how weight is distributed across the sole with every step. Uneven loading patterns, particularly a shift toward the heel or the outside edge, are early signs of compensatory gait under fatigue.

Balance

Left and right load distribution

SmartStep continuously measures how evenly weight is split between the left and right foot. A consistent imbalance, even a subtle one, indicates the worker is favouring one leg and their gait is becoming asymmetric.

Biomechanics

Pronation and walking asymmetry

The degree to which the foot rolls inward with each step, and the symmetry of the stride pattern between left and right, are both tracked continuously. Together they form a sensitive early-warning composite for cumulative fatigue.

Performance

Walking speed and cadence

Walking speed is a well-established indicator of overall physical condition. A worker whose pace has dropped significantly by mid-shift is operating at a higher risk than their morning baseline, even if no incident has occurred yet.

Fatigue

Double support time

The proportion of the gait cycle spent with both feet on the ground rises as the central nervous system compensates for tiredness. This is one of the most clinically established indicators of fatigue-related fall risk available from insole sensing.

Overall score

Composite gait score

All six metrics are combined into a single score that updates throughout the shift. Workers see their own number in real time. Managers see colour-coded scores for every active worker on their dashboard, instantly showing who needs attention.

Location and geofencing

Know where every worker is. Warn them before they enter danger.

SmartStep's GPS layer operates continuously throughout each shift, independently of the main gait pipeline. Location data updates every 10 seconds to the organisational dashboard and is stamped server-side for a full, auditable location trail if an incident occurs.

For workers

  • Full-screen live map

    A Google Maps view centred on the worker's current position, with pinch-to-zoom, drag-to-pan, and satellite view. Before a session starts, it confirms that GPS is locking their position accurately.

  • High-risk zone overlays

    Areas designated as hazardous by the organisation are displayed as highlighted polygons on the map. Workers can see exactly where the danger zones are relative to their current position before they get close.

  • Haptic geofence alerts

    When a worker approaches a designated hazard boundary, the insole and phone vibrate automatically. No screen interaction required. The alert fires whether the worker is looking at the map or not.

  • Lone worker reassurance

    Knowing their location is being recorded and monitored gives lone workers in remote or high-risk environments a tangible safety net, independent of any incident occurring.

For managers

  • Live fleet tracking map

    The organisational dashboard embeds a live Google Maps view showing every active worker as a GPS pin, updating every 10 seconds. H&S managers can see their entire workforce at a glance, including anyone approaching a flagged zone.

  • Automatic high-risk zone creation

    When incidents are logged, their GPS coordinates are recorded. Areas where multiple incidents cluster are automatically flagged as high-risk zones on the organisational map, building a data-driven picture of where on site is most dangerous over time.

  • GPS audit trail

    Every coordinate is server-timestamped. When an incident occurs, investigators have a complete, legally defensible record of exactly where the worker was and which direction they were heading in the minutes before it happened.

  • Incident location pinning

    Every incident record includes a precise GPS pin on the map. When an emergency alert fires, the organisation receives the worker's coordinates immediately, enabling a targeted extraction response rather than a site-wide search.

Incident response

From the moment of detection to a complete legal record, in seconds.

When the insole detects a slip, trip or fall, a chain of automated actions begins. The worker is alerted physically. Emergency contacts are notified with their location. A 10-second biomechanical snapshot is captured and locked to the incident record. The whole process completes before a human has had a chance to react.

What happens on detection

  • Insole and phone vibrate within 2 seconds

    The physical alert fires on both the insole and the smartphone simultaneously. On a construction site or factory floor where ambient noise makes audio alerts useless, this is often the only channel that gets through.

  • 10-second kinematic snapshot locked

    The insole captures the full biomechanical picture of the 10 seconds surrounding the event and transmits it to the app in a structured burst. This snapshot is the foundation of every incident report and root cause analysis.

  • Emergency contacts receive a GPS alert within 10 seconds

    Designated contacts get an automatic notification containing the worker's precise coordinates. For a lone worker on a remote site with no one nearby, this is the difference between a quick extraction and a prolonged emergency.

  • A permanent, tamper-proof record is created

    The incident is written to the cloud with a server-side timestamp, GPS pin, and full kinematic data. It cannot be edited from any device and can be exported for RIDDOR reporting within the legally required timeframe.

What the manager's incident report contains

  • Heel velocity and balance chart

    A time-series plot of heel contact velocity against centre of mass velocity across the 10-second window. The relationship between these two values reveals whether the event was a slip, a near-miss, or a fall.

  • Incident load concentration heatmap

    A frozen snapshot of plantar pressure distribution at the moment of the event. Heel-dominant loading suggests a slip. Forefoot or toe-dominant loading points toward a trip. A lateral spike indicates an ankle inversion.

  • Bilateral toe clearance chart

    Left and right toe clearance values plotted across the preceding strides. A narrowing trend on one side in the build-up to the incident is a reliable indicator that fatigue was a contributing factor.

  • Stride asymmetry chart

    The symmetry of the stride pattern across the full shift, showing whether the incident was an isolated event or the culmination of progressive gait deterioration that had been developing for hours.

About us

Your feet know more than you think.

SmartStep is a collaboration between V12 Footwear and a University of Bath engineering team. Six subsystems. One boot. A single mission: get every worker home safely.

Why this exists

A £22.9 billion problem that starts at your feet.

Workplace injury in Great Britain is not a rare edge case. It is a persistent, measurable, and largely preventable crisis. The boot is the worker's primary contact point with the environment that causes it.

Current safety footwear meets every standard required of it. It resists impact, puncture, and compression. But it does none of this proactively. It cannot monitor whether the worker wearing it is fatiguing, approaching a hazard zone, or about to lose their footing. The foot is the worker's primary contact point with the ground, and it has been producing safety data that no one has been collecting until now.

The partnership

V12 Footwear set the brief. We built the answer.

V12 Footwear is a British family-run safety footwear manufacturer founded in 1998, headquartered in Chippenham, Wiltshire. Built by brothers Andy and Ben Turner as fifth-generation shoemakers, V12 has grown into a globally recognised brand supplying EN ISO 20345-certified footwear to industries including oil and gas, construction, and manufacturing. Their clients include BP, Rolls-Royce, Honda, Sky, and London Underground.

V12 holds ZeroCarbon® certification from the Sustainable Future Group, reflecting a commitment to sustainability that extends into product development. Their brief to our team was straightforward in its ambition and significant in its technical challenge.

"Our feet are the key connection point between our bodies and the ground. Our challenge is to build a method of connecting their movement with data to unlock solutions to slips, trips and falls, temperature control, and posture."

Commercial viability

The market is ready. The product gap is real. The numbers add up.

The market for smart safety wearables is growing fast, and the smart footwear segment is the least developed category within it. No existing product combines real-time gait monitoring, pressure sensing, GPS tracking, and incident detection in a single safety boot. We have costed the full journey from working prototype to scaled commercial launch.

What it costs to make, and what it sells for

Manufacturing cost falls significantly as production volume grows. The electronics are the biggest cost driver at low volume, but become far more affordable at scale through bulk component purchasing and automated assembly. Three scenarios show how the numbers change.

Early prototype £369

per pair to produce. High cost reflects small quantities and hand assembly. Useful for testing and validation, not intended for sale at this stage.

1,000-unit launch £190

per pair to produce. Volume purchasing and semi-automated assembly halve the electronics cost. This is the recommended first commercial launch scenario.

5,000-unit batch £139

per pair to produce. Full production scale with dedicated assembly at V12. Custom-manufactured sensor arrays replace off-the-shelf components, cutting sensor costs by 80%.

Pricing and break-even

SmartStep is sold to employers and safety managers, not directly to individual workers. This is deliberate: the safety data SmartStep generates is most valuable to the organisation responsible for keeping workers safe, and procurement decisions in construction, oil and gas, and manufacturing happen at company level.

A recommended launch price of £349.99 per pair generates a positive return on each unit sold, while remaining well within employer PPE budgets. Adding a £5 per month subscription for access to the safety analytics platform, break-even on the full investment is reached at roughly 1,800 pairs sold. For higher-risk sectors like oil and gas where the safety case is strongest, a higher price point of £429.99 reduces break-even to under 1,200 pairs.

What it costs to get to market

The total investment needed to take SmartStep from its current working prototype to a certified product ready for commercial sale is estimated at around £180,000. This covers tooling, software, product certification, and a paid pilot with real employers.

Environmental impact and sustainability

A full cradle-to-grave environmental assessment was carried out covering every stage of the product's life, from sourcing raw materials through to disposal. The total carbon footprint is roughly equivalent to a long-haul flight per pair over a two-year working life.

Where the impact comes from

Manufacturing the components, not using them

Over 90% of the product's environmental footprint comes from manufacturing the electronic components, not from running them. The good news is that the electronics are tiny and the boot runs on less power than a small LED bulb. Future component choices can meaningfully reduce this figure.

Day-to-day running

Almost nothing once it's in use

Charging the boot once per shift across a two-year working life contributes less than 4% of the total carbon footprint. As the UK electricity grid gets cleaner, this falls further with no changes to the product at all.

Built to last longer

Replace the part, not the whole boot

The battery and electronics slide out for replacement. When they reach the end of their life, the employer replaces only those components rather than the entire boot. This reduces waste, lowers the buyer's long-term cost, and creates a natural repeat revenue stream for V12.

End of life

A take-back scheme as a selling point

Batteries and circuit boards require responsible recycling. A V12 take-back programme turns that obligation into a commercial differentiator, reinforcing the brand's ZeroCarbon credentials while keeping competitors without recycling infrastructure at a disadvantage.

The engineering team

Six subsystems. One product.

SmartStep was developed by a six-person Mechanical Engineering team at the University of Bath, working directly with V12 Footwear. Each engineer owned a distinct technical subsystem. All six had to integrate into a single coherent product built on V12's Bison IGS VR600.01 platform.

Subsystem 1

System Integration

Responsible for the connective framework across all six subsystems, defining the interfaces where power, data, and physical loads are exchanged. This work determined the pressure sensor configuration, battery selection and placement, haptic actuator location, wiring routing, and the overall insole layer stack. Without a robust integration strategy, the individual subsystems cannot function as a coherent product.

FSR pressure matrix Li-Ion battery LRA haptic actuator System architecture
Subsystem 2

Outsole Redesign

Responsible for redesigning the heel and forefoot tread geometry of V12's existing IGS sole to strengthen passive slip resistance. The work benchmarked the certified Bison slip performance against EN ISO 20345 and ISO 13287, identified lubricated steel flat as the critical failure condition, and used material screening alongside FEA comparison of tread concepts to select a geometry and material that improves SRC performance without compromising antistatic properties, weight, or certification routes.

Slip resistance (SRC) FEA tread analysis EN ISO 20345 IGS integration
Subsystem 3

Temperature and Moisture

Responsible for both passive environmental regulation and active monitoring of in-shoe thermal and moisture conditions. Each foot can produce 14 to 57g of sweat per hour. Prolonged moisture build-up degrades skin integrity, promotes infection, and reduces concentration during high-risk tasks. The subsystem integrates a moisture-wicking thermally dissipative insole layer, a replaceable absorption pad, and a thermistor feeding real-time temperature data to the MCU.

Thermistor sensing Moisture-wicking insole SAP absorption pad Microclimate management
Subsystem 4

Electronics and Sensing

Responsible for the IMU, GPS module, and signal-conditioning electronics that fuse all sensor outputs at the microcontroller. This is the intelligence layer that transforms raw physical signals into the gait metrics and incident flags the app depends on. This subsystem specifies the MCU architecture, BLE radio configuration, sensor fusion algorithms, and the sealed electronics enclosure that must withstand IP67 ingress conditions while fitting within the arch of the boot.

6-axis IMU GPS module nRF52840 MCU and BLE Sensor fusion
Subsystem 5

Companion App

Responsible for the Flutter companion app that serves as the display layer for all sensor data and the safety management interface for both individual workers and H&S managers. The app implements a BLOC-driven architecture, BLE GATT connection pipeline, dual Firestore and Hive data strategy, real-time plantar pressure heatmap, GPS fleet tracking with geofenced high-risk zones, RIDDOR-compliant incident logging with four biomechanical precursor charts, and a two-role access control system.

Flutter / Dart Firebase and Firestore BLE GATT pipeline RIDDOR incident log GPS geofencing
Subsystem 6

Manufacturing and Materials

Responsible for translating the overall system concept into a physically realisable product within V12's existing production framework. This work covers the magnetic gasket battery closure mechanism, cable routing and pogo pin connector architecture, smart insole assembly and integration sequence, outsole cementing process, end-of-line quality control including 100% IP67 seal testing, lifecycle cost analysis, and supply chain considerations.

Magnetic gasket closure Pogo pin connectors IP67 QC protocol Carbon lifecycle audit
